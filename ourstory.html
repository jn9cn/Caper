It all started a simple idea

That all chefs should have a platform to share their story.

Sandesh Reddy, a food-loving engineer turned self-taught chef, always had a love for learning and food. Constantly experimenting with flavors common and obscure led to many messy countertops, but ultimately led to his empire of popular restaurant concepts in Chennai (he was the first to successfully introduce ramen there).

His understanding of food cultures and reinterpretation of them with local ingredients drew a crowd whose enjoyment of his signature dishes were enhanced by his story. They were more open to trying new foods and enjoyed the experience he offered them. In essence, he created his own platform to share his passion which helped grow his following and further fueled his love of food.

It dawned upon him that many food purveyors may not have opportunities to share their own culinary inspirations with their eaters. He also realized many eaters were more open to trying new dishes when they craved specific flavors in that dish.

Putting his engineering and epicurean thinking caps together, he sought a solution. Purveyors should be able to tell their story and grow a dedicated following. Users should be able to eat amazing dishes they’re in the mood for.

And like all good startup stories, Caper was born.
